## PART 1: COMPETITOR MAP

### Tier 1: Direct Competitors (overlap on function + audience)

These are the companies Premise AI will compete against for keywords, mindshare, and deals. None of them combine all three of Premise's functions as a managed service, but they each own a piece.

**Scale:** 25,000+ properties, 2.5B sqft
**Website headline:** "The #1 AI COI Tracking Software"
**CTA:** "Talk to Sales"
**Keywords they own:** COI tracking software, certificate of insurance management, insurance compliance automation, vendor compliance
**Tone:** Professional, benefit-driven. Positions as category leader (#1).
**URL:** https://getjones.com

## PART 2: DESIGN DIRECTION & BRAND INSPIRATION

---

### PRIMARY DESIGN REFERENCE: Viteplus.dev

**URL:** https://viteplus.dev/
**This is the design language we're building to.** Alexander approved this as the visual target.

#### Design System to Replicate:

**Layout:**
- Centered column layout with max-width constraints
- Full-width hero with centered text
- Sections flow vertically with alternating alignment (left/centered) for rhythm
- Multi-column feature grids (3-4 cols desktop, 1 col mobile)
- Consistent gap sizing (24-32px)

**Typography:**
- Clean modern sans-serif hierarchy
- Large, dominant headline in hero
- Progressively smaller weights for subsections
- Generous line-height for readability
- Monospace for any technical/data elements (SLA numbers, pricing)

**Color:**
- Minimal palette: primarily dark grays/black for text, white backgrounds
- Strategic accent color (TBD - pick something that reads "commercial real estate trust" not "startup energy")
- Subtle grays for secondary hierarchy
- Light-mode-first with potential dark mode

**Spacing:**
- Generous vertical spacing between sections (60-100px margins)
- Consistent horizontal padding (24-32px mobile, scaling up desktop)
- Whitespace as a design element - sections breathe

**Hero:**
- Large headline, centered or left-aligned
- Subheading describing the core value
- Primary CTA buttons (two: "Start your pilot" + "See how it works")
- Clean, no visual clutter
- Full viewport width, 60-80vh height

**Navigation:**
- Sticky/fixed header
- Logo left, nav items center/right
- Semi-transparent or glass-morphism on scroll
- Clean, minimal

**Cards & Feature Blocks:**
- Rounded corners (8-12px)
- Subtle shadows for elevation (not borders)
- Icon/image header + headline + descriptive text
- Consistent padding

**Buttons:**
- Solid filled for primary, ghost/outline for secondary
- Generous padding (12-16px vertical, 24-32px horizontal)
- Rounded corners matching cards
- Smooth hover transitions (200-300ms)
- Subtle scale or opacity change on hover

**Interactions:**
- Smooth scroll behavior
- Fade-in animations on scroll-into-view
- Minimal, purposeful - never overwhelming
- Logo rows with consistent sizing, slight opacity/grayscale until hover

**Backgrounds:**
- Predominantly white/off-white
- Subtle section contrast through barely-perceptible background shifts
- No jarring color blocks
- Possible subtle dot patterns or gradients (very light)

**Overall feel:** Minimalist, professional, confident. Content clarity over decoration. Whitespace as a feature. "Approachable yet powerful." No skeuomorphism, no excessive ornamentation. Scale, spacing, and subtle shadows for visual definition.

#### How This Applies to Premise AI:

The Viteplus aesthetic is developer-tool-grade polish applied to a service business. For Premise:
- The minimalism communicates competence (this buyer is tired of flashy SaaS dashboards)
- The whitespace communicates calm (this buyer is overwhelmed)
- The typography hierarchy communicates clarity (this buyer needs to understand fast)
- The subtle interactions communicate professionalism (this buyer is making a real ops decision)

Adapt the palette away from dev-tool blues toward something that reads as "institutional real estate" - think deep navy, warm grays, maybe a muted green or copper accent. The structure and spacing translate directly.
